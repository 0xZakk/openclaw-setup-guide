---
title: Voice Profile
description: Capture your writing voice so your agent can ghostwrite in your style
---

A `VOICE.md` file that captures how you actually write -- your patterns, phrases, tone, the whole thing. When your agent writes tweets, articles, or emails for you, it references this file so the output sounds like you, not like ChatGPT.

Generic AI writing is painfully obvious. It uses the same filler phrases, the same structures, the same "let's dive in" energy. A voice profile teaches your agent what you actually sound like. The test is simple: would you actually say this?

## Setup Prompt

```
I want you to build a voice profile for me. Analyze these writing
samples and create a VOICE.md file that captures:

- Core voice traits (e.g., direct, casual, educational)
- Sentence patterns I actually use
- Structure patterns (how I organize thoughts)
- Phrases I say vs. phrases to NEVER use
- The "vibe" -- how would a friend describe my writing?
- AI patterns to specifically avoid (list the cliches)
- A test: before writing anything for me, check
  "would I actually say this?"

Include good and bad examples showing the difference between
my voice and generic AI voice.

[Paste 20-50 of your tweets, posts, or writing samples here]
```

## What Makes a Good Voice Profile

1. **Real examples** -- actual quotes from your writing, not descriptions of your style
2. **Anti-patterns** -- specific phrases to avoid ("Here's the thing:", "Let's dive in", "Game changer")
3. **The vibe test** -- a quick gut-check to see if output sounds right
4. **Format-specific notes** -- tweets sound different from articles sound different from emails

## Tips

- Feed it at least 20 samples. More data = better profile.
- Include variety -- tweets, longer posts, casual messages.
- Update it every few months. Your voice evolves.
- Reference it everywhere -- tweet pipeline, article drafts, email templates, all of it.

## Set Your Expectations

Fair warning: this one takes time. The default AI writing voice is baked deep into these models and a VOICE.md file won't just overwrite it. You'll create the profile, ask your agent to write something, read it, and immediately think "nope, that's not how I talk." That's normal.

What actually works is iteration. You go back and forth on drafts, flag the stuff that sounds off, and over time the voice profile gets sharper. Your agent starts to learn what you'll reject. The output gets closer to your actual voice. But it's gradual -- not a one-shot thing.

And honestly, even with a strong voice profile, you're still going to edit. It gets you maybe 80% of the way there. That last 20% -- the part that makes it sound like a human wrote it and not a very well-prompted LLM -- that still comes from you. Read it, tweak what feels off, cut the parts that don't land. That's just how ghostwriting works, even with human ghostwriters.
