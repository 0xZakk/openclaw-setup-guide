## What It Does

A `VOICE.md` file that captures your unique writing patterns, phrases, tone, and style. When your agent writes tweets, articles, emails, or drafts for you, it references this file to sound like *you* -- not like AI.

## Why It Matters

Generic AI writing is obvious. It uses the same filler phrases, the same structures, the same "let's dive in" energy. A voice profile teaches your agent what you actually sound like so it can produce drafts that pass the "would I actually say this?" test.

## Setup Prompt

```
I want you to build a voice profile for me. Analyze these writing
samples and create a VOICE.md file that captures:

- Core voice traits (e.g., direct, casual, educational)
- Sentence patterns I actually use
- Structure patterns (how I organize thoughts)
- Phrases I say vs. phrases to NEVER use
- The "vibe" -- how would a friend describe my writing?
- AI patterns to specifically avoid (list the cliches)
- A test: before writing anything for me, check
  "would I actually say this?"

Include good and bad examples showing the difference between
my voice and generic AI voice.

[Paste 20-50 of your tweets, posts, or writing samples here]
```

## What Makes a Good Voice Profile

The best voice profiles include:

1. **Real examples** -- actual quotes from your writing, not descriptions
2. **Anti-patterns** -- specific phrases to avoid ("Here's the thing:", "Let's dive in", "Game changer")
3. **The vibe test** -- a quick heuristic to check if output sounds right
4. **Format-specific notes** -- tweets sound different from articles, which sound different from emails

## Tips

- **Feed it at least 20 samples** -- the more data, the better the profile
- **Include variety** -- tweets, longer posts, casual messages
- **Update it** -- your voice evolves. Refresh the profile every few months.
- **Reference it everywhere** -- point your tweet pipeline, article drafts, and email templates at VOICE.md
