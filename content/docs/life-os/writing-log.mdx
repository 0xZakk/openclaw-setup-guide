## What It Does

A cron job that scans your vault for active drafts and outlines, then lists them at the top of today's journal. When you sit down to write, you immediately see what's in progress and can pick something to work on.

## Template

```markdown
## Morning Writing Log
### Active drafts and outlines:
  - [[Draft Title 1]] -- status note
  - [[Draft Title 2]] -- needs editing
  - [[Outline: Article Idea]] -- outline complete, ready to draft
### Pick 1-2 to work on today:
  -
```

## Setup Prompt (Cron Job)

```
Create a cron job called "Morning Writing Log" at 5:00 AM every day.

It should:
1. Search the vault for pages with type:: draft, type:: outline,
   or type:: article
2. Also check workspace for any draft files
3. Filter to only active/in-progress items (not published or archived)
4. Prepend a "Morning Writing Log" section to today's journal listing
   each draft with its status
5. Include: "Pick 1-2 to work on today:"
6. If no active drafts found, write "No active drafts found. Start
   something new?"
7. Log completion in today's memory file

This should be the FIRST section in the daily journal. Prepend,
don't append.
```

## Why 5 AM?

It runs before you wake up so the list is ready when you start writing. Adjust the time to fit your schedule -- the point is it's there before your writing session.
